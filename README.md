# 🍎 Apple Global Sales Analysis (2022–2024)

**End-to-end sales analysis and interactive dashboard built entirely in Microsoft Excel.**

> Analysed $18.04M in global revenue across 11,500 transactions, 6 regions, 4 customer segments, and 6 product categories over three years — surfacing pricing, return, and geographic insights through pivot analysis and an interactive dashboard.

## 🔍 Key Findings

1. **Mac Pro drives 20.6% of total revenue** — $3.72M from just 546 units ($6,821 avg/unit), the highest revenue concentration of any single product.

2. **iPhone & iPad have the highest return rates** — iPhones at 8.28% and iPads at 8.27%, with Third-Party Retailer (8.62%) the worst-performing channel.

3. **Android switchers are the #1 iPhone buyer cohort** — 407 units (19.4% of known-prior-OS buyers), representing a significant acquisition opportunity.

4. **High discounts reduce revenue without driving volume** — 11–15% discounts deliver $1,201 avg order value vs $1,634 at 0–2%, a 26.5% drop with no meaningful unit uplift.

5. **Europe + Asia = 63.9% of global revenue** — Europe leads at $6.21M (34.4%), Asia at $5.43M (30.1%). Africa and Oceania are under-penetrated.

6. **Q4 consistently peaks** — October 2024 was the single highest month at $671,886, driven by the iPhone 15 launch cycle.

7. **All 4 customer segments are near-equal** — Individual (25.7%), Government (25.7%), Education (25.5%), Business (23.1%): a diversified, low-concentration base.

8. **Customer ratings are uniformly stable** — All categories rate between 3.97–4.04 out of 5, suggesting consistent satisfaction across the product range.

---

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations

- **Cap discounts at 5–7%** — redirect savings into bundle or loyalty incentives that grow basket size rather than reduce unit price.
- **Launch Android switcher campaign** — trade-in programme targeting the largest identifiable iPhone acquisition cohort.
- **Audit reseller return processes** — Third-Party Retailer and Authorized Reseller channels have the highest return rates; pre-sale education and QC checks are needed.
- **Build a Q4 playbook** — formalise seasonal inventory, staffing, and marketing plans to capture consistent Q4 demand acceleration.
- **Invest in Africa & Oceania** — both regions are underserved; a phased distribution strategy could meaningfully diversify the revenue base.
